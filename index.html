About Me

I'm currently pursuing my PhD at Chalmers University of Technology, where I focus on vehicle engineering and autonomous systems. My research revolves around the creation and use of high-quality datasets for the development of autonomous maritime vehicles. I'm particularly interested in how these datasets can help advance sensor technologies and improve benchmarking for robotics systems.

Research Interests

  • Autonomous maritime systems
  • Sensor fusion: electro-optical, radar, sonar, visual, and inertial
  • SLAM and visual odometry for marine vessels
  • Benchmarking and reproducibility in robotics

Recent Projects

  • REEDs (Robot Perception Benchmark Dataset)
    A collaborative project focused on creating a large-scale, reproducible benchmarking dataset to accelerate perception research in robotics. The REEDs dataset includes challenging scenarios across environments, sensor modalities, and platforms to support robust system development.
  • EPA (Enhanced Perception Ashore)
    A project aimed at improving perception and situational awareness for autonomous systems by adapting land-based sensors like stereo cameras, lidar, and radar for maritime applications. This work contributes to fusing diverse sensor data into cohesive and dependable perception models.
  • OpenDLV
    An open-source, microservice-based software framework for autonomous systems and robots. OpenDLV facilitates communication between software components and hardware devices, enabling scalable and modular development of autonomous vehicles.
  • Multi-Vessel Control for Unmanned Surface Vessels
    Participated in a project under the WARA-PS initiative, focusing on developing control strategies for coordinating multiple unmanned surface vessels. The goal was to enable collaborative operations among autonomous maritime platforms.
